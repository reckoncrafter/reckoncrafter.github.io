The Hippocratic Oath of Software Developers

I swear by Athena, Hermes, and Hephaestus, making them my witnesses, that I will carry out, according to my ability and judgement, this oath and this indenture.

I vow to uphold the values of user freedom, and commit myself to creating software that aims to make the world a better place for all of its inhabitants.

I vow never to write software that unfairly takes advantage of users and their devices.

I vow to be transparent about the behavior and goals of my software applications, such that users have the freedom to decide if using my software is in their best interest, based on accurate and truthful information.

I vow to treat all identifying information from users as sacred, and not to be used against the will of users to enrich myself or any enterprise I associate with, and to make transparent the use of such information as part of my software.

By taking this oath, I acknowledge, as a software developer, that I hold power to do great good, and power to do great evil. I acknowledge that I have the choice to use computer technology to profit from deceiving users, or to advance freedom, justice, and equality on a global scale, and vow to pursue the latter.

I recognize my ability, as a software developer, to create applications that do real harm, and vow to do no harm above all else, and assert that I be condemned should I break this oath.
